## What I Build

I build **open scientific infrastructure for molecular discovery**: datasets, benchmarks, AI/ML workflows, cheminformatics pipelines, spectroscopy tools, and research software that connect computational predictions back to real chemistry.

My work sits at the intersection of:

<table>
<tr>
<td width="33%"><b>Medicinal Chemistry</b><br>QSAR, SAR, docking, transporter biology, lead optimization</td>
<td width="33%"><b>Molecular Sensing</b><br>Fluorescent probes, spectroscopy, chemometrics, analytical chemistry</td>
<td width="33%"><b>Molecular AI</b><br>Machine learning, active learning, uncertainty, open benchmarks</td>
</tr>
</table>

> The future of molecular discovery will be built not only by better algorithms, but by better scientific infrastructure.

## The JAI Lab Manifesto

- Better datasets beat bigger claims.
- Benchmarks turn ideas into measurable progress.
- Experiments should be machine-readable.
- Molecular AI must remain connected to chemistry.
- Open infrastructure accelerates discovery.
- Scientific software should be reproducible, documented, and useful.
