# Honors and recognition display strategy

This file exists because the awards are not decoration; they are credibility signals. They should appear near the top of the public GitHub profile and should not be buried under skills or repository lists.

## Primary recognition set

| Recognition | Year | Positioning value |
|---|---:|---|
| UCSF PBBR Postdoctoral Independent Research Award | 2024 | Independent research funding; high-risk, high-reward biomedical research |
| Harvard Business School Foundry Bootcamp | 2026 | Scientific entrepreneurship, commercialization, investor-pitch readiness |
| ACS Postdoc to Faculty Workshop | 2025 | Faculty-track competitiveness and academic leadership preparation |
| Sigma Xi Full Member | 2026 | Recognition by a major scientific honor society |
| Excellence in Doctoral Research Award | 2022 | Doctoral research distinction |
| Special Sponsor Award, NCCR Bioinspired / Swiss Academy of Sciences | 2022 | International young-chemist recognition |

## Where these now appear

- root `README.md` recognition line under the hero
- root `README.md` dedicated `Recognition and honors` section
- `assets/svg/recognition-wall.svg` visual recognition wall
- organization profile README
- website starter
- `content/profile_facts.yml` structured profile facts
- minimal and institute-style alternative READMEs

## Tone rule

Use these honors confidently, but do not overclaim. The best framing is:

> Recognition across independent biomedical research funding, faculty-track preparation, scientific entrepreneurship, doctoral excellence, and international chemistry programs.

Avoid phrases like `world-renowned`, `top scientist`, or `leading global expert` unless independently supported by external evidence.
